Protecting Sensitive Data in Machine Learning

Are you concerned about the security of your machine learning models when handling sensitive information?
Our Machine Learning Evaluator is designed to put your worries to rest by testing out various de-identification techniques.
But what exactly is de-identification?

Understanding De-Identification

De-Identification is the process of removing or modifying personally identifiable information (PII) from datasets, while still retaining their analytical utility. The goal is to safeguard privacy and adhere to data protection regulations while ensuring that the data remains useful for analysis. Our evaluator explores a variety of de-identification methods, from simple masking to advanced anonymization, to see which ones have the most significant impact on security without sacrificing model performance.

We examine how these techniques can balance privacy protection with accurate model outcomes, ensuring your models are both secure and effective.
